# ☕️Bright coffee shop

# 🔎Overview
This portfolio showcases a data-driven Sales Performance Analysis for Bright Coffee Shop. Utilizing SQL, miro,canva,excel and Databricks, I transformed six months of raw transactional data into actionable business insights and strategic presentation materials.

# 🎯Aim
My goal is to translate six months of transactional data into a Strategic Growth Framework. By analyzing purchase patterns and category velocity, this project identifies specific opportunities to boost average transaction value

# 📈key Insights
Store perfomence: Revenue is distributed almost equally across all locations:Hell's kitchen 34%, Store Astoria 33%, and Manhattan 33%. This reflects uniform brand strength and consistent customer demand across the entire portfolio.

product:The Barista Espresso is the shop's primary revenue driver at 13%. Combined, the top four products account for 44% of total sales, highlighting a strong customer preference for core Coffee and Tea categories over specialty add-ons.

Morning Lead: Over half of all revenue (56%) is generated before noon.

Evening Slump: Revenue drops significantly to 9%, indicating a commuter-heavy customer base

Weather Impact: Sales peak in May/June as colder temperatures drive coffee consumption

Overoll growth:sales generally goes up from january to june on all stores

# 💡GROWTH AND  Recommendations
Bulk:Introduce combos in all stores and mostly in the morning introduce bulk buy only in manhatta

May and June: Create hot sales campaign from  may to june

Morning Combo Launch: Implement curated breakfast pairings across all locations to capitalize on the 56% morning peak and drive higher transaction values.

Manhattan Bulk-Buy Pilot: Introduce high-volume "Office Bundles" exclusively at the Manhattan store to serve the local corporate demographic and increase bulk revenue.

Tuesday Value Campaign: Launch a recurring "Mid-Week Special" to incentivize customer visits during off-peak days.

Objective: Stabilize daily revenue fluctuations and increase store foot traffic by 15–20% on traditionally slower weekdays.
